Lasagna a la Frija

Just like my mother still sometimes makes

Ah, lasagna...

Garfield's, my sister's and probably a lot of many people's favorite dish. There is something about beef ragu sandwiched between lasagna sheets and delicious sauce that just hits the spot! As kid, my parents had a busy schedule, juggling work and extracurriculars. It was common to eat frozen lasagna every few weeks when they couldn't find the time to cook, and my sister and I loved it! But occasionally, on birthdays or the rare instance my dad was off work at lunch time on a weekend, mom would make her special lasagna from scratch and today, as an adult, I still haven't found a taste quite like it.

My father is the cook at home, but lasagna is my mom's specialty. Enjoy!

Bechamel sauce

  • 1.5 l milk (half/fat)
  • 50 g of butter
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • Black pepper (to taste)
  • Nutmeg (to taste)
  • Kosher salt (to taste)

Assembly

  • 1 package lasagna sheets
  • Slices of cheese (enough to cover the tray)
  • 1 fresh mozzarella
  • Oregano (to taste)

Preparation

Beef ragu

In Portuguese we call this "carne a bolonhesa"
  1. Finely chop the onion and mince the garlic. Peel and chop the carrot into tiny cubes (like mille-poix).
  2. Coat the whole bottom of a large pot with olive oil and place on medium heat. Once hot, add the onions, garlic, carrot, bay leaf and red chilli. Let sizzle until translucent.
  3. Add the ground beef and break it apart with a large spoon or spatula. Add some salt (not too much!), paprika and cumin and mix well into the meat. Let the meat sear for around 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the canned tomatoes with the juice and mix well. If you use whole tomatoes, crush them with your hand or with a spatula.
  5. Finally, fill the same can with red wine until about 4/5 of the capacity and add to the pot. Keep heat on medium level and cover with a lid.
  6. Once it starts boiling, remove the lid and let simmer until the sauce is reduced to your liking. Taste and add more salt or seasoning as needed.
  7. To finish, add the basil leaves and chopped parsley and cover with the lid.

Bechamel sauce

  1. Place the butter on a pot a bring to medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and incorporate with a whisk for a few minutes.
  2. Gradually incorporate the milk, stirring, always.
  3. Add the seasonings and leave on medium heat until the sauce is thickened. Do a taste test and adjust the seasoning as needed, and keep stirring to prevent the sauce from gluing to the bottom

Assembly

  1. In casserole dish, spread 2 tablespoons of bechamel sauce.
  2. Cover the bottom with lasagna sheets; you can break some apart if needed.
  3. Fill the first layer with a generous amount of the ragu (about 1 cm thick).
  4. Finish the layer by pouring bechamel sauce over all the meat and cover again with lasagna sheets.
  5. Repeat these steps until you are satisfied with the layer number. I think 3 to 4 layers is the best.
  6. To finish, cover the top layer with the cheese slices and spread the fresh mozarella bits; sprinkle some oreagno on top for garnish
  7. Bake at 200°C until the top is browned.

Enjoy!
